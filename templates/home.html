Main Types of Lung Cancer Nodules

Benign Nodule

A benign lung tumor is a non-cancerous growth in the lungs or airways. Benign lung tumors usually don't cause symptoms, but if they do, they might include coughing, wheezing, or shortness of breath.

Prevention Steps: Avoid smoking, avoid hazardous chemicals, eat a healthy diet.

Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer (NSCLC)

NSCLC accounts for 85% of lung cancers. It includes subtypes such as adenocarcinoma, squamous cell carcinoma, and large cell carcinoma.

Prevention Steps: Avoid smoking, reduce exposure to secondhand smoke and pollutants

Small Cell Lung Cancer (SCLC)

SCLC is less common, accounting for about 15% of cases. It is aggressive and tends to spread more quickly.

Prevention Steps: Quit smoking, regular health checkups, and awareness of family history can help in early detection and prevention.

Adenocarcinoma

Lung adenocarcinoma is a type of NSCLC that begins in the glandular cells lining the alveoli of the lungs.

Prevention Steps: Quit smoking, regular health checkups, and awareness of family history can help in early detection and prevention.

Squamous Cell Carcinoma (SCC)

SCC originates in the flat cells lining the lungs' airways. It's the second most common type of NSCLC.

Prevention Steps: Quit smoking, regular health checkups, and awareness of family history can help in early detection and prevention.

Malignant

Malignant lung cancer is a cancerous tumor in the lungs that can grow into nearby tissue and spread to other parts of the body.Treatment depends on the stage of the disease and can include surgery

Prevention Steps: Quit smoking, regular health checkups, and awareness of family history can help in early detection and prevention.
